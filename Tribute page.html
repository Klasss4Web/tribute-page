DORA AKUNYILI:THE IRON LADY

PROF. DORA AKUNYILI

BIOGRAPHY

Dora Akunyili was born in Makurdi, Benue State of Nigeria to Chief and Mrs. Paul Young Edemobi in July 1954. She is a native of Agulu in Anambra State.She had her West African School Certificate Examination (WASCE) in Queen of Rosary Secondary School, Enugu State, where she graduated with Grade 1 Distinction. She won the Eastern Nigerian Post Primary Scholarship and the Federal Government of Nigeria Undergraduate Scholarship. After her Secondary Education, he gained admission into University of Nigeria Nsukka where she bagged her first degree(B.Pharm, 1978), and a PhD in 1985.

In 1988, Dora lost her sister, Vivian who died taking fake insulin injection, an event that would greatly shape her life. After the death of Vivian, Dora came to the forefront of the war against drug counterfeiters and in 2001 she was appointed the Director-General of National Agency for Food Administration and Control (NAFDAC) of Nigeria until 2008.During her reign as NAFDAC boss, she recorded enomous success in war against fake drugs which earned her international recognition and awards both at home and abroad.

She was the Nigerian Minister of Information and Communication from 2008 to 2010. during her time as Minister of Communication, she averted what would have been a bad precedence and a constitutional crisis when she spoke publicly on what the constitution states in a scenario where the president is incapicitated or out of seat for ninety days. this led to backlash from members of the presidency and her eventual resignation as Minister of the federal Republic of Nigeria.

She won several awards(over 700) for her work in Pharmacology, public health and human rights. She died in an Indian hospital on 7 June 2014 after a battle with utherine cancer.
